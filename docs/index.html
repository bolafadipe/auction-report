SEPTEMBER 2024 - WATCH AUCTION ANALYSIS

Introduction

The September 2024 New York Watch Auctions marked a significant moment in the luxury timepiece market, showcasing an exceptional collection of watches from renowned brands and attracting global attention. This report delivers a detailed analysis of the auction outcomes, highlighting the distribution of sales across various dimensions including brands, models, case materials, and bracelet types. By examining data from two prominent auction houses—Phillips and Christie's—we offer valuable insights into current market dynamics, emerging trends, and the performance of leading watch brands during this pivotal month.

Methodology

The data presented in this report was collected from auction results published by Phillips and Christie's, covering the period of the September 2024 New York auction season. Our comprehensive breakdown encompasses total lots offered, overall sales figures, and achieved sell-through rates, providing a nuanced understanding of the market's strength and buyer preferences. This report identifies standout timepieces that garnered significant interest, reflecting evolving collector tastes and investment patterns in the luxury watch sector.

Limitations

Due to our inability to source some of the data used in the report, we were unable to provide a full replica of the original report. As such, differences will exist between our results and those of the original report.

Auction House Performance

Phillips accounted for the majority of sales, with a total of $5,861,429 (69%), while Christie's achieved $2,635,902 (31%). Phillips demonstrated stronger demand across more lots, showing its ability to attract higher bidding activity.

Top Brands by Total Sales

Patek Philippe led total sales by brand, followed by Rolex and Audemars Piguet. Patek Philippe watches achieved the highest sales, underscoring their ongoing desirability among collectors. Rolex also performed strongly, contributing significantly to the overall total.

Top Models by Total Sales

The top-selling model was the Patek Philippe Nautilus, which led sales by model. Rolex’s Cosmograph Daytona and Audemars Piguet’s Royal Oak models were also top performers, drawing strong interest from buyers.

Total Sales by Case Size

The 40mm case size was the most popular, contributing the highest amount to total sales. Case sizes like 42mm and 36mm also performed well, reflecting a mix of modern and classic preferences among buyers.

Total Sales by Case Material

Stainless steel dominated case material sales, followed closely by 18K pink gold and 18K white gold. Platinum and other precious metals also contributed significantly to the total.

Total Sales by Bracelet Material

Leather bracelets, particularly crocodile, accounted for the largest share of sales by bracelet material. This was followed by stainless steel and ceramic bracelets, which cater to different market segments, from classic to modern sports watches.

Total Sales by Category

Modern watches contributed the majority of sales by category, driven by their broad appeal and popularity among collectors. Independent brands, though contributing a smaller share of total sales, had the highest average sale price, reflecting their rarity and craftsmanship.

Summary

The September 2024 watch auctions demonstrated a robust market for luxury timepieces, with Phillips leading the auction in total sales and lots sold above estimate. Patek Philippe and Rolex emerged as top brands, with modern watches and stainless steel materials seeing the strongest demand. Independent brands continued to command high prices per piece, indicating a growing niche market for artisanal and rare timepieces. Overall, the auction results reflect a healthy, competitive market with strong collector interest.
