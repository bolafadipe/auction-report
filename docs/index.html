JUNE 2024 - HYPEBEAST VERSION

Introduction

The 2024 New York Auction Season showcased an impressive array of luxury watches from top-tier brands, setting new benchmarks in sales performance. This report provides a comprehensive analysis of the auction results, detailing the sales distribution by brand, model, case materials, bracelet types, and auction houses. Drawing from two leading auction houses—Phillips and Christie's—this report offers key insights into market trends, demand drivers, and the performance of top brands during the auction season.

Methodology

The data presented in this report was collected from auction results published by Phillips and Christie's, covering the period of the 2024 New York auction season. Each auction result was meticulously analyzed, focusing on total sales, number of lots sold, and performance against price estimates. Our analysis covers key categories such as brand, model, case materials, and bracelet materials, alongside a comparison of auction house performances. The data-driven insights presented herein offer a valuable perspective on market dynamics during this high-profile auction season.

Limitations

Due to our inability to source some of the data used in the report, we were unable to provide a full replica of the original report. As such, differences will exist between our results and those of the original report.

Brand Dominance

Rolex and Patek Philippe are the two undisputed leaders in terms of total sales, with Rolex narrowly outpacing Patek Philippe by a slight margin. Their combined sales account for nearly half of the total sales among the top 10 brands. Rolex led the auction sales with an impressive total of $5.5 million, closely followed by Patek Philippe with $5.3 million. F.P. Journe also had a significant showing, contributing over $3 million in total sales. Cartier and Audemars Piguet made up the top five, confirming their strong demand in the luxury watch market. The data also reveals that the market for vintage and independent watch brands, like Philippe Dufour and Charles Frodsham, remains robust among collectors.

Key Reasons for Their Dominance:

  • Market Reputation: Both Rolex and Patek Philippe have solidified their position as the epitome of luxury and craftsmanship over decades. Rolex, known for its precision, durability, and timeless design, appeals to both experienced collectors and newcomers. Patek Philippe’s reputation is deeply rooted in tradition and the artisanal quality of its timepieces, making it a favorite among those seeking heritage and legacy.
  • Historical Prestige and Collector Demand: Patek Philippe's rarity and exclusivity, combined with its generational watchmaking expertise, makes it a popular choice for elite collectors. Rolex, on the other hand, has an extensive history with its iconic models (e.g., Daytona, Submariner) that drive demand from both collectors and aspirational buyers.
  • Brand Desirability: These two brands have built global desirability. Rolex, with its sport-luxury appeal, is often considered a symbol of success, while Patek Philippe is associated with exclusivity and refined taste.

Combined Sales Comparison: With total sales of nearly 10M USD between them, Rolex and Patek Philippe dominate the market. Their combined sales overshadow those of all other brands, demonstrating the broad market appeal these two brands command. Their ability to capture both the high-end collector’s market and casual luxury buyers makes them industry leaders in terms of both volume and value.

Insights on the Luxury Watch Market

The sales data indicate several clear trends within the luxury watch market:

  • Heritage and Brand Legacy Matter: The dominance of brands like Rolex and Patek Philippe shows a strong preference for classic, long-established brands with a proven track record of delivering value and prestige. The consistent performance of F.P. Journe, Audemars Piguet, and Cartier further emphasizes the appeal of well-established brands with deep market penetration.
  • Independent Watchmaking on the Rise: The presence of smaller, independent brands such as Philippe Dufour and Richard Mille highlights growing demand for watches that offer uniqueness and craftsmanship over mass production. Collectors increasingly value bespoke watchmaking, with independent artisans gaining a stronger foothold in the auction market.
  • Rarity and Innovation Drive Premium Prices: Brands with limited production numbers or groundbreaking innovation tend to capture higher prices at auction. This is particularly true for F.P. Journe, Richard Mille, and Philippe Dufour, where scarcity and innovative design make them highly sought after.

Potential Growth Areas

Based on current performance, several brands show potential for future growth:

  • Richard Mille: Its combination of futuristic design, innovative materials, and celebrity endorsements positions it for continued growth. If it can maintain its momentum in the luxury space, it could challenge traditional brands in future auctions.
  • Philippe Dufour and Charles Frodsham: These independent watchmakers, while operating on a small scale, could see their value rise as collectors increasingly seek unique, hand-made timepieces. Growing demand for independent brands in the luxury watch market indicates that niche watchmakers could see significant appreciation in future auctions.
  • Audemars Piguet and A. Lange & Söhne: Both brands have the pedigree and craftsmanship to continue growing their market share. A focus on limited-edition collections and complicated timepieces could further bolster their standing in the auction market.

Model Dominance

The Cosmograph Daytona models dominate the top 10 list with the "Paul Newman John Player Special" leading total sales at 952,500 USD. The dominance of Daytona models, including other versions like the "Paul Newman Panda" and the standard Cosmograph Daytona, shows the continued desirability of this iconic model. Their strong performance indicates the lasting impact of these vintage models on both new collectors and seasoned auction participants.

Key Reasons for Their Dominance:

  • Heritage and Iconic Status: The Cosmograph Daytona, especially the Paul Newman versions, are synonymous with horological history and prestige. These watches are not only tied to a famous personality but also represent one of the most desirable categories of vintage Rolex.
  • Collector Demand: Both the rarity of certain Paul Newman variations and the increasing demand for vintage Rolex watches drive significant interest. As vintage Rolex continues to trend upwards, the Cosmograph Daytona maintains a strong position at auctions.
  • Brand Desirability: Rolex's overall brand power and the allure of its limited-edition, historically significant pieces fuel demand. The combination of Rolex’s brand identity and the Daytona’s racing heritage ensures its value retention and increase over time.

Compared to other models in the top 10, Cosmograph Daytona models consistently capture the highest auction prices, reinforcing their position as the ultimate collector’s watch. The strong sales of the various Daytona iterations far exceed those of other models, showing the enduring popularity of Rolex’s most famous chronograph model.

Variety and Rarity

The top 10 list reflects a broad range of both iconic and rare timepieces. Watches like the Simplicity and Tourbillon Souverain are highly regarded for their technical complexity and limited availability, both contributing to their strong market performance at 533,400 USD. Collectors gravitate toward these models due to their exceptional craftsmanship and exclusivity, with independent watchmakers like Philippe Dufour offering highly sought-after bespoke creations.

Rarity is a key factor for these top-tier models, where limited runs and impeccable craftsmanship drive high auction prices. In contrast to mass-produced luxury watches, these rare pieces appeal to the discerning collector who values uniqueness and technical expertise over brand recognition alone.

Role of Limited Editions and Special Editions

The prominence of limited-edition watches such as the Paul Newman Cosmograph Daytona reflects the strong demand for unique, historically significant timepieces. Limited production runs and specific variations, such as the “Paul Newman Panda,” have led to competitive bidding, further elevating their market value.

Collectors place a premium on the scarcity and cultural legacy of such watches, especially those that are no longer in production. The role of exclusivity in determining auction prices cannot be overstated, with these special editions commanding significant premiums due to their rarity and provenance.

Innovation and Craftsmanship

Models like the Simplicity, Chronomètre à Résonance, and Centigraphe Souverain distinguish themselves through horological innovation and meticulous craftsmanship. These watches exemplify technical brilliance and creativity, which appeal to a more niche audience of collectors looking for independent brands and artisanal work.

These models, often created by individual watchmakers such as Philippe Dufour or F.P. Journe, are recognized for their mechanical innovation and artistry. Their presence among the top 10 demonstrates how technical excellence can compete with mass brand appeal at auctions.

Market Insights

The auction results indicate that collectors continue to value both historical icons like the Cosmograph Daytona and rare, technically innovative models like the Simplicity and Tourbillon Souverain. This dual interest highlights the strength of the luxury watch market, where both vintage appeal and modern craftsmanship are highly sought after.

  • Heritage Brands Lead: Rolex remains the top player due to its extensive history and iconic status in the luxury watch industry. Patek Philippe and F.P. Journe follow closely with technically exceptional timepieces that continue to impress collectors.
  • Independent Watchmaking Grows: The presence of brands like Philippe Dufour and F.P. Journe highlights the rising appreciation for independent artisans. As more collectors seek unique, limited-production timepieces, independent watchmakers are gaining momentum in the high-end market.
  • Rarity and Innovation Drive Premium: Highly limited models or those featuring groundbreaking technical achievements remain among the most desirable, commanding the highest auction prices.

Potential Growth Areas

Looking at future auctions, several models show significant growth potential:

  • Nautilus: As Patek Philippe continues to phase out certain models of the Nautilus, scarcity is expected to drive demand and future prices higher.
  • Richard Mille: The combination of cutting-edge design and limited availability positions Richard Mille for future growth, especially among younger collectors seeking modern luxury.
  • Independent Brands: Philippe Dufour, F.P. Journe, and other independent watchmakers are likely to see increased demand as more collectors seek out bespoke, artisanal timepieces that provide both technical excellence and rarity.

Top Case Materials Analysis

Case Size Dominance

The 40mm case size leads total sales with nearly 4M USD in total sales, significantly outpacing all other case sizes. This dominance indicates a strong preference among collectors and buyers for watches with a more versatile, classic size that appeals to a broad range of wrist sizes.

Other popular case sizes include 42mm and 37mm, with total sales reaching approximately 2.6M USD and 2.3M USD, respectively. These sizes also reflect the balance between modern trends favoring larger watches and the traditional appeal of more moderately sized timepieces.

Key Reasons for the Dominance of 40mm:

  • Versatility and Comfort: The 40mm case size is widely regarded as the ideal watch size for a range of wrist sizes, making it a popular choice among both men and women. It is large enough to be legible and modern without being too oversized.
  • Iconic Models: Many iconic watch models, such as the Rolex Submariner and Cosmograph Daytona, are produced in the 40mm size, driving its popularity at auctions. These highly sought-after models contribute significantly to the overall sales figures for this case size.
  • Historical Preference: Historically, 40mm has been a standard size for luxury watches, offering a perfect blend of elegance and practicality. This long-standing popularity continues to influence buyer preferences today.

Variety in Case Sizes

The top 10 case sizes show a clear preference for mid-sized to large watches. While the top three sizes (40mm, 42mm, and 37mm) dominate the chart, there is still significant interest in sizes ranging from 36mm to 41mm, each of which accounts for a notable share of total sales.

Smaller sizes such as 36.5mm and 35mm still hold relevance in the luxury watch market, often appealing to buyers who prefer more classic or vintage-inspired designs. These sizes also tend to be favored by collectors looking for understated elegance rather than bold, oversized watches.

Insights on the Luxury Watch Market

The data reflects a balanced market that caters to both modern tastes for larger watches and more traditional preferences for moderately sized timepieces. The enduring popularity of 40mm watches demonstrates a classic appeal that transcends temporary trends, while the presence of 42mm and larger sizes reflects modern consumers' interest in bold, statement-making designs.

  • Modern Trends Favor Larger Sizes: The demand for case sizes like 42mm and 41mm highlights the growing interest in larger, sportier designs that appeal to a more casual, adventurous clientele.
  • Classic Sizes Remain in Demand: While modern buyers may lean towards larger watches, classic sizes such as 37mm and 36mm are still highly desirable, especially among collectors and those seeking vintage models.
  • Continued Relevance of Traditional Sizes: Even smaller sizes like 35mm remain relevant, often driven by vintage market demand and a preference for watches with a more understated, timeless appeal.

Potential Growth Areas

Looking ahead, several trends may shape future sales by case size:

  • Increasing Popularity of Larger Watches: As modern consumers continue to favor larger watches, sizes like 42mm and above may continue to see strong sales. However, buyers looking for sportier, more adventurous designs are likely to focus on these case sizes.
  • Continued Demand for Classic Sizes: Case sizes between 37mm and 40mm are likely to remain highly sought after, especially as more brands reissue vintage models or produce timepieces that appeal to traditional watch enthusiasts.
  • Growth in Smaller Case Sizes: As the vintage market continues to grow, the demand for watches with case sizes below 37mm may also increase, particularly among collectors who prioritize classic designs over modern trends.

Top Bracelet Materials Analysis

Bracelet materials played a significant role in driving total sales during the auctions. Crocodile leather straps led the way, generating over $5.62 million, followed by leather straps with $4.75 million. Surprisingly, watches with no defined bracelet or strap type also performed well, totaling over $2.82 million in sales. 18K yellow gold Rolex Oyster bracelets were another standout, bringing in $952,500. Collectors continue to value watches that feature high-quality materials, whether luxurious gold bracelets or classic leather options.
