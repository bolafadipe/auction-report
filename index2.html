Introduction

The September 2024 New York Watch Auctions marked a significant moment in the luxury timepiece market, showcasing an exceptional collection of watches from renowned brands and attracting global attention. This report delivers a detailed analysis of the auction outcomes, highlighting the distribution of sales across various dimensions including brands, models, case materials, and bracelet types. By examining data from two prominent auction houses—Phillips and Christie's—we offer valuable insights into current market dynamics, emerging trends, and the performance of leading watch brands during this pivotal month.

Methodology

The data presented in this report was collected from auction results published by Phillips and Christie's, covering the period of the September 2024 New York auction season. Our comprehensive breakdown encompasses total lots offered, overall sales figures, and achieved sell-through rates, providing a nuanced understanding of the market's strength and buyer preferences. Additionally, this report identifies standout timepieces that garnered significant interest, reflecting evolving collector tastes and investment patterns in the luxury watch sector. Through this analysis, we aim to equip watch enthusiasts, industry professionals, and market observers with crucial information to navigate the complex and ever-changing landscape of high-end watch auctions.

Sales and Average Price by Category

Modern Dominance

Modern watches dominate total sales, accounting for the vast majority of revenue. This indicates a strong preference for contemporary timepieces among auction buyers. Vintage watches follow as the second-highest category in terms of total sales.

Independent Watches

When looking at average sale price, Independent watchmakers lead significantly. This suggests that while fewer Independent watches may be sold, they command premium prices, likely due to their rarity and craftsmanship.

Key Insights:

  • Modern Watches Appeal to a Broader Audience: The high total sales for modern watches reflect their broader market appeal, attracting both new buyers and seasoned collectors.
  • Independent Watchmakers Hold the Value: Despite lower sales volume, independent brands command a much higher average price due to their artisanal quality and rarity.
  • Vintage and Neo-Vintage Interest: These categories show lower total sales and average prices compared to Modern and Independent categories, but they still represent significant segments of the market, indicating continued interest in historical and collectible timepieces.

Brand Dominance

Patek Philippe leads the auction sales, followed closely by Rolex. Audemars Piguet, F.P. Journe, and Cartier round out the top five brands in terms of total sales. This ranking reflects the continued dominance of traditional luxury watch brands in the auction market.

Key Reasons for Their Dominance:

  • Market Reputation: These top brands have solidified their position as the epitome of luxury and craftsmanship over decades.
  • Historical Prestige and Collector Demand: Rarity, exclusivity, and historical significance drive demand from elite collectors.
  • Brand Desirability: These brands have built global desirability, often considered symbols of success and refined taste.

Top Models by Total Sales

Model Dominance

The Nautilus model from Patek Philippe leads in total sales, followed by the Cosmograph Daytona from Rolex. The Aquanaut, another Patek Philippe model, ranks third. Audemars Piguet's Royal Oak models (both the Perpetual Calendar Black Ceramic and the Openworked Perpetual Calendar) also feature prominently in the top-selling models.

Key Insights:

  • Iconic Models Lead: Classic models with strong brand heritage dominate sales, reflecting their enduring appeal to collectors.
  • Complexity and Rarity Drive Value: Models with complex mechanisms or limited availability tend to achieve higher sales figures.
  • Brand Synergy: The top-selling models align closely with the top-selling brands, reinforcing the strong market position of these luxury watchmakers.

Top Case Sizes Analysis

Case Size Preferences

The 40mm case size dominates total sales, followed by 42mm and 36mm sizes. This indicates a preference for mid-sized watches among auction buyers, with a slight trend towards larger sizes as evidenced by the strong performance of 42mm cases.

Key Observations:

  • Versatility of 40mm: The popularity of 40mm cases suggests a sweet spot that appeals to a wide range of wrist sizes and style preferences.
  • Trend Towards Larger Sizes: The strong showing of 42mm cases may indicate a growing preference for slightly larger watches, possibly driven by contemporary fashion trends.
  • Continued Relevance of Classic Sizes: The presence of 36mm among the top sizes shows that classic, more modest case sizes still have a significant following.

Top Case Materials Analysis

Case Material Preferences

Stainless steel leads in total sales among case materials, followed by 18K pink gold and 18K white gold. Platinum and various types of gold (18K, white, yellow, and pink) make up the majority of the top-selling case materials, reflecting the enduring appeal of precious metals in luxury watches.

Key Insights:

  • Popularity of Stainless Steel: The dominance of stainless steel suggests a preference for durable, versatile watches that balance luxury with practicality.
  • Precious Metals Remain Strong: The strong showing of various gold types and platinum indicates that traditional luxury materials continue to be highly valued by collectors.
  • Diversity in Precious Metals: The presence of different gold colors (yellow, white, pink) in the top materials shows a diverse taste in precious metal aesthetics.

Top Bracelet Materials Analysis

Bracelet Material Preferences

Leather bracelets lead in total sales, followed closely by crocodile leather. This demonstrates the continued popularity of traditional, luxurious strap materials in high-end watches. Stainless steel Rolex Oyster bracelets and ceramic Audemars Piguet bracelets also feature prominently, indicating strong sales for these iconic bracelet designs from well-known brands.

Key Observations:

  • Traditional Materials Dominate: The preference for leather and exotic leather straps underscores the enduring appeal of classic luxury in watch design.
  • Brand-Specific Bracelets: The strong performance of specific bracelet types like the Rolex Oyster and Audemars Piguet ceramic bracelets highlights the importance of iconic designs in driving sales.
  • Balance of Luxury and Practicality: The mix of leather straps and metal bracelets in the top-selling materials suggests that buyers value both luxurious aesthetics and everyday wearability.

Auction House Performance Overview

The table above compares the performance of two major auction houses, Phillips and Christie's, during the September 2024 auction. Phillips significantly outperformed Christie's in terms of total sales, with Phillips accounting for 69% ($5,861,429 USD) of the total sales compared to Christie's 31% ($2,635,902 USD).

Sale Performance

Both auction houses achieved impressive sale rates, with Phillips reaching a perfect 100% sale rate and Christie's achieving 100% as well. This indicates exceptionally strong demand across the board and accurate market valuation of the lots.

Lots Sold Above, Within, and Below Estimate

Overall, 40.7% of lots (155) were sold above their estimated prices, 43.3% (165) within estimate, and 16.0% (61) below estimate. This distribution shows a healthy market with competitive bidding for many items, while still providing opportunities for buyers at various price points.

Insights on Auction House Strategy and Market Trends

The performance data suggests that both auction houses were successful in attracting serious collectors and high-end buyers. The high percentage of lots sold above estimate indicates strong competition for desirable pieces.

  • Phillips' Market Dominance: Phillips' significantly higher total sales suggest they may have had a larger number of high-value lots or were particularly successful in marketing their offerings to wealthy collectors.
  • Christie's Strong Performance: Despite lower total sales, Christie's matched Phillips in sale rate, indicating they were equally successful in finding buyers for their lots, albeit at generally lower price points.

Potential Future Growth Areas

Based on this auction, both auction houses have demonstrated strengths:

  • Phillips: Could focus on maintaining their edge in securing and marketing high-value lots to continue their market leadership.
  • Christie's: Might look into strategies to secure more high-value lots to increase their total sales while maintaining their strong sale rate.

Summary

The September 2024 auction season demonstrated the continued strength of the luxury watch market, with both Phillips and Christie's achieving 100% sale rates. Patek Philippe and Rolex led in brand performance, while modern watches dominated total sales. The auction showed a preference for mid-sized watches, particularly 40mm cases, and a strong demand for both traditional materials like leather straps and precious metals, as well as contemporary materials in iconic designs. The success of independent watchmakers in commanding high average prices highlights the value placed on craftsmanship and exclusivity. Overall, the auction results reflect a diverse and robust market, balancing appreciation for classic luxury with growing interest in contemporary and innovative timepieces.
